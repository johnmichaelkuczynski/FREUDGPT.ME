==========================================
KEY THEORETICAL FRAMEWORKS
==========================================

1. TRAUMA THEORY: Neurotic symptoms originate in inadequately processed traumatic experiences, particularly of a sexual nature in childhood

2. SYMBOLISM: Physical symptoms symbolically encode psychological conflicts and traumatic memories

3. THE UNCONSCIOUS: An unconscious intelligence organizes symptoms and guides associations toward pathogenic material

4. REPRESSION: Pathogenic ideas are kept from consciousness through active defense mechanisms

5. THERAPEUTIC TECHNIQUE: Analysis works by uncovering repressed material through pressure technique, free association, and interpretation

6. SEXUALITY: Childhood sexuality and sexual trauma play central etiological roles in neurosis

7. DEFERRED ACTION: Traumatic experiences gain pathogenic force retroactively through later understanding
