WORK-001: BEYOND THE PLEASURE PRINCIPLE (1920/1922) - 61 POSITIONS
===================================================================

METAPSYCHOLOGY (16 positions): META-001 to META-016
- Pleasure-principle as automatic regulator
- Economic viewpoint in metapsychology
- Pleasure-pain and quantity of excitation
- Principle of constancy
- Limitations on pleasure-principle supremacy
- Reality-principle replaces pleasure-principle
- Repression as source of pain
- Consciousness and memory as incompatible
- System W-Bw at the boundary
- Protective barrier against stimuli
- Trauma as breaking through barrier
- Timelessness of unconscious processes
- Perceptual system and consciousness
- Binding vs free-flowing energy
- Primary vs secondary process
- Metapsychological description of three systems

INSTINCT THEORY (18 positions): INST-001 to INST-018
- Conservative nature of instincts
- All instincts aim to restore earlier state
- Ultimate aim of life is death
- Death-instinct as drive toward inorganic state
- Life-preserving instincts as component instincts
- Enigma of organic life creation
- Two classes of instincts: Eros and Death
- Eros aims to establish greater unities
- Repetition-compulsion beyond pleasure-principle
- Dreams of traumatic neuroses as exceptions
- Play and mastery through repetition
- Fort-Da game as expression of instinct for mastery
- Transference-neurosis repetitions not from pleasure
- Compulsion to repeat more primitive than pleasure
- Repressed material never forgotten
- Repression and resistance as same process
- Destiny neurosis (repetition of fate pattern)
- Demonic character of fate repetition

CLINICAL THEORY (14 positions): CLIN-001 to CLIN-014
- War neuroses and ego conflict
- Traumatic neurosis and fright
- Anxiety as preparation prevents trauma
- Traumatic dreams seek retrospective anxiety
- Physical injury diminishes traumatic neurosis risk
- Transference-neurosis as artificial illness
- Patient repeats rather than remembers
- Transference as playground for repetition-compulsion
- Resistance and repetition from same source
- Overcoming resistance as slow work
- Fate neurosis: character-determined life pattern
- Children's play as mastery of experience
- Repetition in children vs adults
- Infantile narcissistic injury and inferiority complex

LIBIDO THEORY (6 positions): LIB-001 to LIB-006
- Evolution of libido concept
- Narcissistic libido
- Inadequacy of original ego/sexual distinction
- Risk of pansexualism
- Conjugation as prototype of sexual effect
- Nirvana-principle

SPECULATIVE BIOLOGY (7 positions): BIO-001 to BIO-007
- Weismann's distinction: mortal soma vs immortal germ-plasm
- Death as late acquisition in Weismann
- Controversy over protozoan immortality
- Protozoa die from own metabolic products
- Hering's anabolic and katabolic processes
- Schopenhauer: death as real result of life
- Multicellularity as life-prolonging

WORK-002: THREE ESSAYS ESSAY I - SEXUAL ABERRATIONS (1905) - 52 POSITIONS
===================================================================

SEXUAL IMPULSE THEORY (7 positions): SEX-001 to SEX-007
- Sexual impulse analogous to hunger
- Popular assumptions about sexual impulse untrustworthy
- Sexual object and sexual aim as analytical concepts
- Sexual impulse independent of its object
- Sexual impulse permits great variation in object
- Sexual impulse poorly controlled by higher psychic activities
- Nature and value of sexual object secondary

INVERSION THEORY (11 positions): INV-001 to INV-011
- Three types of inversion (absolute, amphigenous, occasional)
- Variations in inverts' self-understanding
- Critique of degeneration theory of inversion
- Neither pure congenital nor pure acquired theory sufficient
- Bisexual predisposition theory
- Psychic and somatic hermaphroditism are independent
- Inversion of character not regular in male inverts
- Critique of 'female brain in male body' theory
- Sexual object of inverts not simply reversed
- Sexual object unites both sex characters
- No uniformity of sexual aim in inversion

PERVERSION THEORY (24 positions): PERV-001 to PERV-024
- Normal sexual aim defined
- Preliminary sexual activities in normal process
- Classification of perversions (anatomical vs temporal)
- Sexual overestimation of object
- Sexual overestimation enables anatomical extension
- Loathing as force opposing libido
- Anal and oral mucous membranes claim status as genitals
- Fetishism and sexual overestimation
- Pathological fetishism defined by fixation
- Fetishism and early sexual impressions
- Symbolic thought in fetish formation
- Touching and looking as intermediary sexual aims
- Covering of body and sexual inquisitiveness
- When looking becomes perversion
- Active and passive forms of perversions
- Sadism as independent aggressive component
- Masochism as transformation of sadism
- Sadism and masochism regularly coexist
- Paired contrasts in perversions
- Perversions as universal components of normal sexuality
- Exclusivity and fixation define pathological perversion
- Psychic participation in perversions
- Shame and loathing as psychic forces confining sexual impulse
- Sexual impulse as composite not simple

NEUROSIS THEORY (10 positions): NEUR-001 to NEUR-010
- Psychoneuroses based on sexual motive powers
- Symptoms as sexual activities
- Hysterical character: sexual repression beyond normal
- Hysteria as contrasting pair: immense craving and exaggerated rejection
- Neurosis as evasion of conflict
- Neurosis as negative of perversion
- All neurotics show unconscious inversion
- Anatomical transgressions active in neurotic symptoms
- Partial impulses (especially cruelty) as symptom-creators
- Love-hate transformation via union of cruelty with libido

WORK-003: INTERPRETATION OF DREAMS SECTIONS II-VIII (1900) - 60 POSITIONS
===================================================================

DREAM WORK THEORY (9 positions): WORK-001 to WORK-009
- Condensation as core dream-work mechanism
- Displacement as transvaluation of psychical values
- Dramatization as presentation requirement
- Dream-work preserves logical relations through formal characters
- Dream absurdity expresses disagreement and scorn
- Secondary elaboration creates dream fa√ßade
- Dream-work is not creative
- Composite and mixed formations in dreams
- Overdetermination of dream elements

CENSORSHIP THEORY (5 positions): CENS-001 to CENS-005
- Repression as cause of dream distortion
- Displacement serves to disguise repressed content
- Censorship operates between two psychic systems
- Sleep weakens but does not eliminate censorship
- Dream as compromise between systems

DREAM SYMBOLISM (6 positions): SYMB-001 to SYMB-006
- Sexual symbols as universal dream language
- Dreamers do not know meaning of their symbols
- Symbolism not created by dream-work
- Specific sexual symbols in dreams
- Bisexual tendency of dream symbolism
- Symbol interpretation requires context

WISH THEORY (10 positions): WISH-001 to WISH-010
- All dreams fulfill unconscious infantile wishes
- Three classes of dreams by wish-relation
- Counter-wish dreams still fulfill wishes
- Unconscious wishes are always active and indestructible
- Day remnants require unconscious reinforcement
- Recent impressions necessary for dream formation
- Dream as hallucinatory wish-fulfillment
- Dream is fragment of abandoned psychic life of child
- Hysterical symptoms as double wish-fulfillments
- All psychoneurotic symptoms are wish-fulfillments

DREAM FUNCTION (6 positions): FUNC-001 to FUNC-006
- Dream as guardian of sleep
- Dream removes sleep disturbances by wish-fulfillment
- Sleep state enables dream formation
- Dream interprets sensory stimuli to preserve sleep
- Consciousness directed to dreams throughout sleep
- Dream as safety-valve for unconscious

ANXIETY DREAM THEORY (5 positions): ANX-001 to ANX-005
- Anxiety dreams as failed wish-fulfillments
- Dream anxiety as transformed libido
- Anxiety dream as punishment dream
- Childhood sexual experiences and adult anxiety dreams
- Pavor nocturnus as incomprehensible sexual feelings

PRIMARY SECONDARY PROCESS (12 positions): PROC-001 to PROC-012
- Primary process as original psychic activity
- Secondary process as later development
- Primary process characteristics
- Secondary process seeks thought-identity
- Secondary process inhibits primary discharge
- Principle of pain regulates both systems differently
- Thinking as detour from hallucinatory wish-fulfillment
- Primary processes dominate when thoughts abandoned by foreconscious
- Consciousness as sensory organ for psychic qualities
- Sleep diminishes foreconscious but not unconscious activity
- Repression occurs when fulfillment would produce pain
- Complex mental operations possible without consciousness

REGRESSION THEORY (6 positions): REGR-001 to REGR-006
- Three types of regression in dreams
- Regression serves dramatization
- Hallucinatory character through regression
- Regression path follows memory structures
- Regression enabled by sleep state
- Regression not unique to dreams

WORK-004: THE FUTURE OF AN ILLUSION (1927) - 209 POSITIONS
===================================================================

CULTURE THEORY (23 positions): CULT-001 to CULT-023
- Culture has two aspects: knowledge/power over nature AND regulation of human relations
- The two tendencies of culture are interdependent
- Every individual is virtually an enemy of culture
- Culture must be defended against the individual
- Culture's institutions and laws directed toward self-defense
- Culture imposed on resisting majority by minority with means of power
- Every culture must be built on coercion and instinctual renunciation
- No certain advance in regulation of human affairs despite advance in conquering nature
- Reorganization removing coercion would be golden age but questionable if realizable
- All men have destructive, anti-social, anti-cultural tendencies
- Critical question is diminishing/reconciling/compensating instinctual sacrifices
- Impossible to do without government of masses by minority
- Coercion indispensable for work of civilization
- Masses are lazy and unintelligent
- Masses have no love for instinctual renunciation
- Masses not convinced by argument
- Individuals in masses support each other in unruliness
- Masses need leaders who set examples
- Two human characteristics require coercion: men not fond of work, arguments ineffective against passions
- Limitations of man's capacity for education set bounds to cultural transformation
- Certain percentage of mankind will always remain asocial
- Resources themselves not essence of culture but means of defending culture
- Psychical sphere of culture includes coercive measures and means to reconcile/recompense

INSTINCTUAL PROHIBITION THEORY (14 positions): INST-019 to INST-032
- Instinct cannot be satisfied = frustration
- Means securing frustration = prohibition
- Condition from prohibition = privation
- Earliest instinctual prohibitions: incest, cannibalism, murder
- These prohibitions began culture thousands of years ago
- Earliest prohibitions still operative, form kernel of hostility to culture
- Instinctual wishes born anew with every child
- Neurotics react to first frustrations with asocial attitude
- Strength of incest wishes still perceived behind prohibition
- Murder still practiced under certain conditions in our culture
- External compulsion gradually internalized in development
- Super-ego as special mental function taking compulsion under jurisdiction
- Internalization of super-ego highly valuable for culture
- Degree of internalization differs widely by prohibition

RELIGION THEORY (28 positions): REL-001 to REL-028
- Religious ideas as most precious possession of culture
- Religious ideas prized as most valuable thing culture offers
- Men suppose life intolerable without according religious ideas their claimed value
- Religious ideas in broadest sense = illusions
- Humanization of nature makes impersonal forces manageable
- Elements given passions like our own souls
- Death as violent act of evil Will, not spontaneous
- Supernatural beings resemble those of our environment
- Substitution of psychology for natural science provides immediate relief
- Humanization has infantile prototype in parental relation
- Mother as first protection against anxiety
- Father soon replaces mother as protector
- Father both danger and protection (ambivalence)
- Adult makes forces of nature into gods with father characteristics
- Gods retain threefold task: exorcise terrors, reconcile to fate/death, amends for suffering
- Gods' domain gradually shifts - morality becomes their real domain
- Laws of culture claimed to be of divine origin
- Life serves higher purpose in religious system
- Soul as object of elevation and exaltation
- Intelligence superior to us orders everything for good
- Benevolent Providence watches over each one
- Death not annihilation but beginning of new existence
- Moral laws govern whole universe with supreme judicial court
- All good rewarded, all evil punished (if not in life, then after)
- Life after death continues earthly existence
- God as single person recovers intimacy of child-father relation
- Religious ideas gone through long development process
- Contemporary Christian culture in west represents one phase of development

ILLUSION THEORY (15 positions): ILL-001 to ILL-015
- Illusion not same as error, not necessarily an error
- Illusion derived from men's wishes
- Illusion approaches psychiatric delusion but distinguished from it
- Delusion emphasizes conflict with reality as essential
- Illusion need not be false, unrealizable, or incompatible with reality
- Belief called illusion when wish-fulfillment prominent factor
- Illusion disregards relations to reality
- Religious doctrines are all illusions
- Religious doctrines do not admit of proof
- No one can be compelled to consider religious doctrines true
- Some religious doctrines so improbable, comparable to delusions
- Reality value of most religious doctrines cannot be judged
- Scientific work only way to knowledge of external reality
- Illusion to expect anything from intuition or trance
- Intuition/trance give only particulars about own mental life

RELIGIOUS EPISTEMOLOGY (10 positions): EPIS-001 to EPIS-010
- Three arguments for religious belief: ancestors believed, proofs from antiquity, forbidden to question
- Prohibition on questioning has only one motive: society knows uncertain basis
- Ancestors far more ignorant, believed things we cannot accept
- Proofs from texts bear every trace of untrustworthiness
- Religious texts full of contradictions, revisions, interpolations
- Divine revelation assertion is itself part of doctrines to be examined
- No statement can bear its own proof
- Religious doctrines have weakest claim where most significant
- Impossibility of proving religious doctrines felt at all times
- Spiritualism fails to prove immortality of soul

WISH-FULFILLMENT THEORY (8 positions): WISH-011 to WISH-018
- Religious ideas fulfill oldest, strongest, most insistent wishes
- Secret of religious strength is strength of wishes
- Terrifying effect of infantile helplessness aroused need for protection
- Father relieved infantile helplessness through love-protection
- Discovery that helplessness continues through life necessitates clinging to father
- Benevolent providence allays anxiety about life's dangers
- Moral world order ensures fulfillment of justice demands
- Future life provides local/temporal setting for wish-fulfillments

FATHER COMPLEX THEORY (9 positions): FATH-001 to FATH-009
- Longing-for-father explanation identical with need for protection
- Child's defensive reaction gives features to adult's reaction (religion formation)
- Object-choice follows anaclitic type
- Libido attaches to objects ensuring satisfaction
- Mother as first love-object and protection against anxiety
- Father replaces mother, situation persists through childhood
- Relation to father affected by peculiar ambivalence
- Father himself a danger because of earlier mother relation
- Father feared no less than longed for and admired

TOTEMISM AND ORIGINS (10 positions): TOT-001 to TOT-010
- Totem and Taboo not intended to explain origin of religions but of totemism
- First form of protecting deity was animal
- Prohibition against killing/eating totem animal existed
- Solemn custom to kill and eat totem communally once yearly
- Totem animals become sacred animals of gods
- Murder prohibition and incest prohibition originate in totemism
- Animal god eventually replaced by human god
- Primal father as prototype of God
- God influenced origin of murder prohibition, not social insight
- Religious doctrine gives historical truth in remodelled/disguised form

HISTORICAL TRUTH THEORY (7 positions): HIST-001 to HIST-007
- Religious ideas contain wish-fulfillments AND important historical memories
- Combination of past and present gives religion matchless power
- Once before helplessness existed: as child in relation to parents
- Primal father murder evoked irresistible emotional reaction
- From primal murder arose commandment: thou shalt not kill
- Men determined to respect father's will after brutally setting him aside
- Religious stock contains historical residue plus wish-illusions

NEUROSIS ANALOGY (9 positions): NEUR-011 to NEUR-019
- Religion comparable to childhood neurosis
- Religion as universal obsessional neurosis of humanity
- Child must pass through neurosis phase to complete development
- Child unable to suppress instinctual impulsions by rational effort
- Child checks impulsions by repressions with anxiety motive
- Most child neuroses overcome spontaneously growing up
- Mankind in development experiences conditions analogous to neuroses
- Religion originated in Oedipus complex, relation to father
- Abandoning religion must take place with fateful inexorability of growth

BELIEVER PSYCHOLOGY (5 positions): BEL-001 to BEL-005
- True believer protected against certain neurotic afflictions
- By accepting universal neurosis, believer spared forming personal neurosis
- Believer has ties of affection binding to religion substance
- No believer will be led astray by arguments
- Vast number obey civilization laws intimidated by religious threats

CRITIQUE OF RELIGION (14 positions): CRIT-001 to CRIT-014
- Religion performed great services but not enough
- If religion succeeded, no one would strive to alter conditions
- Appallingly large number discontented with civilization
- Many feel civilization as yoke to be shaken off
- Religion forfeited part of influence due to science advances
- Doubtful men happier when religion held unlimited sway
- Men always externalized religious precepts, frustrating intentions
- Priests made concessions to keep masses submissive
- Religion supported immorality no less than morality
- Religion's achievements in promoting happiness/morality not impressive
- Scientific spirit shows errors in religious documents
- Comparative method reveals fatal resemblance to primitive productions
- Decline of religious belief follows increased knowledge accessibility
- Educated/brain workers' religious motives replaced by secular ones

MASS PSYCHOLOGY (8 positions): MASS-001 to MASS-008
- Masses require leaders who can set examples
- Leaders recognized by masses, induce submission to labor/renunciation
- Leaders should have superior insight into life necessities
- Leaders should have mastered own instinctual wishes
- Danger that leaders yield to masses to maintain influence
- Leaders must be independent via means of enforcing authority
- Neglected classes grudge favoured ones their privileges
- Suppressed classes develop intense hostility to culture

CLASS THEORY (7 positions): CLASS-001 to CLASS-007
- Restrictions applying only to certain classes create obvious injustice
- Suppressed classes intent on destruction of culture itself
- Suppressed classes not prepared to acknowledge cultural prohibitions
- Latent hostility of better-provided classes overlooked
- Culture leaving large number unsatisfied has no prospect of continued existence
- Suppressed can share narcissistic satisfaction of cultural ideal
- Right to despise outsiders compensates for wrongs suffered in own group

INTERNALIZATION THEORY (8 positions): INT-001 to INT-008
- External compulsion gradually internalized in development
- Super-ego takes compulsion under its jurisdiction
- Child presents model of internalization transformation
- Internalization makes child moral and social being
- Strengthening of super-ego highly valuable for culture
- People with strong super-ego become culture supporters
- Greater their number, more secure culture, less external coercion needed
- Degree of internalization differs widely by prohibition

EDUCATION THEORY (12 positions): EDUC-001 to EDUC-012
- Religious education retards development
- Religious education closes important sphere to mental function
- Child introduced to religious doctrines when neither interested nor capable
- Two main points of modern education: retard sexual development, early religious influence
- When child's mind awakens, religious doctrines already unassailable
- Closing sphere to child with hell-threats not conducive to mental strengthening
- Feeble mentality follows accepting absurdities without criticism
- Thought-prohibitions prevent attaining psychological ideal
- Religious thought-inhibition similar to sexual one
- Experiment of non-religious education worth attempting
- If human nature remains same, ready to give up reform
- Believer unable to sleep if deprived of religious consolation like sleeping draught

CULTURAL REFORM (11 positions): REF-001 to REF-011
- Senseless to try to do away with religion by force and at once
- Hopeless to eliminate religion suddenly
- Believer will not let faith be taken by arguments or prohibitions
- Education to reality as sole aim
- Man must learn to endure great necessities with resignation
- By withdrawing expectations from other world, concentrate energies on earthly life
- Life will be tolerable for all when no one oppressed by culture
- Removing God from cultural prohibitions gains advantages
- Admitting human origin of laws makes people more friendly to them
- General revision of cultural laws will involve abolition of many
- Reconciliation to civilization burden far advanced by reform

SCIENCE AND REASON (15 positions): SCI-001 to SCI-015
- Voice of intellect soft but doesn't rest until gains hearing
- After endlessly repeated rebuffs, intellect succeeds
- One may be optimistic about future of mankind in this respect
- Primacy of intellect lies in far but not infinite distance
- Intellect will set same aims as God but within human limits
- Intellect aims at brotherhood and reduction of suffering
- Intellect's realization very gradual, in incalculable future
- In long run nothing can withstand reason and experience
- Contradiction religion offers to reason/experience too palpable
- Purified religious ideas still contradict reason if preserving consolation
- Scientific work can discover something about reality
- Through science can increase power and regulate life
- Science has many open and secret enemies
- Science young with difficult beginnings
- Transformation of scientific ideas is development not revolution

FUTURE PREDICTIONS (8 positions): FUT-001 to FUT-008
- Abandoning religion will take place with fateful inexorability
- We are in middle of this developmental phase
- Man cannot remain child forever, must venture into hostile world
- With liberated energies on earth, life tolerable for all
- No one will be oppressed by culture anymore
- Religious doctrines must be discarded on way to distant goal
- First attempts may fail, first substitutes prove unstable
- Generations without religious education will attain primacy of intelligence
- Culture incurs greater danger maintaining religion than relinquishing it

PHILOSOPHY OF "AS IF" (4 positions): PHIL-001 to PHIL-004
- Philosophy of "As If": behave as if believing fictions for practical motives
- Religious doctrines as fictions for maintenance of society
- Man not influenced by philosophical wiles cannot accept "As If"
- With confession of absurdity/illogicality, no more to be said

CREDO QUIA ABSURDUM (4 positions): CRED-001 to CRED-004
- Credo quia absurdum: doctrines outside reason's jurisdiction
- Religious truth must be inwardly felt, not comprehended
- Credo only voluntary confession, not binding decree
- No obligation to believe every absurdity

CULTURAL IDEALS (5 positions): IDEAL-001 to IDEAL-005
- Cultural ideals as judgements of loftiest accomplishments
- Ideals modelled on first achievements, not determinants
- Narcissistic satisfaction ideal gives based on pride in achievement
- Cultures compare themselves and despise others
- Cultural ideals become source of discord and enmity

ART THEORY (2 positions): ART-001 to ART-002
- Art offers substitutive gratifications for oldest cultural renunciations
- Art promotes feelings of identification in cultural group

NATURE AND FATE (5 positions): NAT-001 to NAT-005
- Nature does not restrain instincts but destroys us coldly, cruelly, callously
- United together and created culture to defend against nature
- Principal task of culture to defend us against nature
- Culture performs defense for everyone in same way
- Unvanquished nature = Fate inflicts evils on individual
