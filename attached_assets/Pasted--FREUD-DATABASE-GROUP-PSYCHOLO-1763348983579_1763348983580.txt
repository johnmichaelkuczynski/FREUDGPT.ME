INDIVIDUAL VS SOCIAL PSYCHOLOGY (7 positions): GSOC-001 to GSOC-007
- Individual psychology always involves others as model/object/helper/opponent
- Individual psychology is simultaneously social psychology in extended sense
- Individual relations (parents, siblings, love-object) are social phenomena
- Narcissistic vs social mental acts fall within individual psychology
- Group psychology concerns influence by large number of people simultaneously
- Group psychology studies individual as member of race/nation/profession/institution
- Social instinct may not be primitive but developed in narrower family circle

LE BON'S GROUP MIND THEORY (18 positions): LEBON-001 to LEBON-018
- Psychological group creates collective mind in members
- Group makes individuals feel/think/act differently than in isolation
- Group is provisional being of heterogeneous elements forming new characteristics
- Individual in group differs fundamentally from isolated individual
- Unconscious phenomena preponderant in group mental life
- Conscious life small in comparison to unconscious life
- Racial unconscious emerges when individual distinctiveness vanishes in group
- Mental superstructure removed, unconscious foundations exposed in group
- Individual in group acquires sentiment of invincible power from numerical considerations
- Anonymity of group eliminates sense of responsibility
- Individual in group throws off repressions of unconscious instincts
- Contagion determines special group characteristics
- Every sentiment and act is contagious in group
- Individual readily sacrifices personal interest for collective in group
- Suggestibility is most important factor determining group characteristics
- Suggestibility produces characteristics contrary to isolated individual
- Individual loses conscious personality in suggestion
- Groups demand illusions and cannot do without them

LE BON ON GROUP CHARACTERISTICS (14 positions): LEBON-019 to LEBON-032
- Groups give unreal precedence over real
- Groups influenced equally by untrue and true
- Groups have evident tendency not to distinguish real from unreal
- Predominance of phantasy rules group psychology like neurosis
- Groups guided by psychological reality not objective reality
- Function for testing reality falls into background in groups
- Groups place themselves instinctively under authority of chief
- Group is obedient herd that cannot live without master
- Group has thirst for obedience
- Group submits instinctively to anyone appointing himself master
- Leader must be held in fascination by strong faith
- Leader must possess strong imposing will group can accept
- Leaders work upon group through fanatical belief in ideas
- Prestige is mysterious irresistible power of domination

MCDOUGALL'S GROUP THEORY (15 positions): MCD-001 to MCD-015
- Contradiction between negative and positive group accounts solved by organization factor
- Unorganized group is "crowd" with no or minimal organization
- Members must have something in common for psychological group
- Common interest, similar emotional bias, reciprocal influence needed for group
- Mental homogeneity determines ease of group formation
- Exaltation or intensification of emotion is most important group result
- Emotions stirred in group to pitch seldom attained otherwise
- Pleasurable to surrender to passions and lose sense of individual limits
- Direct induction of emotion by primitive sympathetic response (emotional contagion)
- Perception of emotional signs automatically arouses same emotion in perceiver
- Greater number with same emotion strengthens automatic compulsion
- Individual loses power of criticism and slips into same emotion
- Mutual interaction intensifies emotional charge of individuals
- Compulsion exists to do same as others and remain in harmony
- Coarser and simpler emotions more apt to spread through group

MCDOUGALL ON GROUP ORGANIZATION (10 positions): MCD-016 to MCD-025
- Group impresses individual with sense of unlimited power and insurmountable peril
- Group replaces human society as wielder of authority
- Perilous to oppose group, safer to hunt with pack
- Individual puts conscience out of action in obedience to group authority
- Surrender to attraction of increased pleasure from removal of inhibitions
- Collective inhibition of intelligence occurs in groups
- Lower intelligence brings down higher intelligence to own level
- Intensification of emotion creates unfavorable conditions for intellectual work
- Individuals intimidated by group, mental activity not free
- Lowering of sense of responsibility for own performances in group

MCDOUGALL'S FIVE CONDITIONS FOR ORGANIZED GROUPS (5 positions): MCD-026 to MCD-030
- Continuity of existence (material or formal) needed for organization
- Individual member must form definite idea of group's nature/functions
- Group should be brought into interaction or rivalry with other similar groups
- Group should possess traditions, customs, habits determining member relations
- Group should have definite structure with specialization of functions

SUGGESTION AND LIBIDO THEORY (8 positions): SUGL-001 to SUGL-008
- Suggestion and hypnosis are fundamental phenomena requiring explanation
- Suggestibility is undefined and unexplained primitive phenomenon
- Le Bon's formulas about suggestion circular and explain nothing
- Suggestion is partial manifestation of hypnotic state
- Hypnosis based on unconscious fixation of libido to hypnotist
- McDougall's principle of direct induction is contagion
- Love relationships (emotional ties) constitute essence of group mind
- Group held together by power of Eros

CHURCH AND ARMY AS ARTIFICIAL GROUPS (12 positions): CHUR-001 to CHUR-012
- Church and army are highly organized lasting artificial groups
- External force prevents disintegration and checks structural alterations
- Illusion exists of head (Christ/Commander) who loves all members equally
- Everything depends upon this illusion of equal love
- Both would dissolve if illusion dropped (barring external force)
- Christ stands as kind elder brother and father surrogate to believers
- Democratic character in Church: all equal before Christ
- Christian community similar to family structure
- Tie uniting each with Christ is cause of tie uniting members
- Commander-in-Chief is father who loves all soldiers equally
- Soldiers are comrades through equal love from commander
- Army built of series of hierarchical groups unlike Church

LIBIDINAL TIE THEORY (8 positions): LIB-007 to LIB-014
- Each individual bound by libidinal ties to leader and other members
- Double libidinal tie (to leader and members) explains lack of freedom in group
- Intense emotional ties in two directions alter and limit personality
- Essence of group lies in libidinal ties existing in it
- Panic occurs when group disintegrates and libidinal ties cease
- In panic mutual ties cease and gigantic senseless dread released
- Panic demonstrates dread from loss of libidinal structure not external danger
- Neurotic anxiety arises from libido withdrawn from its object

TWO FORMS OF EMOTIONAL TIE (6 positions): LIB-015 to LIB-020
- Two forms: identification and substitution of object for ego ideal
- Group members identify with one another through common relation to object (leader)
- Leader or leading idea takes place of ego ideal
- Common quality shared by members lies in nature of tie with leader
- Emotional ties between members secondary to tie with leader
- Army panic: loss of leader causes disruption of all ties

IDENTIFICATION THEORY (17 positions): IDEN-001 to IDEN-017
- Identification is earliest form of emotional tie with object
- Identification is original form of emotional tie to another person
- Identification precedes object-choice
- Identification is ambivalent from beginning, can express tenderness or wish for removal
- Identification plays part in Oedipus complex: boy identifies with father
- Three sources of identification: original emotional tie, regressive substitute for object-choice, new common quality
- Identification differs from object-choice: ego wants to be like object vs wants to have object
- Father as ideal in identification, as obstacle in object-choice (Oedipus)
- Identification remains when object abandoned due to loss or renunciation
- In melancholia identification substitutes for lost object-cathexis
- Identification possible with any new perception of common quality
- More important the common quality, more successful the identification
- Hysterical identification based on wish to put oneself in same situation
- Hysterical imitative identification signals common quality in unconscious
- Identification can arise with any new common quality perceived with non-object
- Group tied together by identification of members with one another
- Identification based on important common quality: nature of tie with leader

JEALOUSY AND GROUP FORMATION (5 positions): IDEN-018 to IDEN-022
- Jealousy transformed into group feeling and identification
- Elder child forced into identification with younger due to parental equal love
- Communal or group feeling grows up from jealousy transformation
- First demand of reaction-formation is justice and equal treatment
- If cannot be favorite oneself, nobody else shall be favorite

SOCIAL FEELING THEORY (6 positions): SOC-001 to SOC-006
- Social feeling (Gemeingeist, esprit de corps) derived from original envy
- Social justice means denying ourselves so others must do without too
- Demand for equality is root of social conscience and sense of duty
- Social feeling based upon reversal of hostile feeling into identification
- Reversal effected under influence of common tender tie with person outside group
- Demand for equality applies only to members not to leader

BEING IN LOVE THEORY (14 positions): LOVE-001 to LOVE-014
- Being in love involves both directly sexual and aim-inhibited instincts
- Depth of love measured by share taken by inhibited instincts of tenderness
- Sexual over-estimation: loved object enjoys freedom from criticism
- All characteristics valued more highly when loved
- Illusion produced that object loved for spiritual merits not sensual charm
- Tendency falsifying judgment is idealization
- Object treated same way as own ego in love
- Considerable narcissistic libido overflows onto object in love
- Object serves as substitute for unattained ego ideal
- Love it for perfections we strove for in own ego
- Object can get possession of entire self-love of ego
- Object has consumed ego in extreme cases
- Traits of humility, limitation of narcissism, self-injury occur in love
- Functions of ego ideal cease to operate in being in love

OBJECT REPLACING EGO IDEAL (6 positions): LOVE-015 to LOVE-020
- Object takes place of ego ideal as formula for extreme love
- In identification ego enriched with object properties (introjection)
- In love ego impoverished, surrendered to object
- In identification object lost or given up, then set up inside ego
- In love object retained with hypercathexis by ego at ego's expense
- Critical distinction: whether object put in place of ego or ego ideal

HYPNOSIS THEORY (11 positions): HYP-001 to HYP-011
- From being in love to hypnosis only short step
- Hypnosis shows same humble subjection, compliance, absence of criticism as love
- Same absorption of own initiative in hypnosis as love
- Hypnotist stepped into place of ego ideal
- Everything clearer and more intense in hypnosis than love
- Hypnotist is sole object with no attention to others
- Ego experiences requests in dream-like way
- Ego ideal in hypnosis stops testing reality of things
- Complete absence of uninhibited sexual tendencies contributes to purity
- Hypnotic relation is devotion in love with sexual satisfaction excluded
- Hypnosis is group formation with two members

LASTING TIES THEORY (4 positions): HYP-012 to HYP-015
- Sexual tendencies inhibited in aims achieve lasting ties between men
- Not capable of complete satisfaction unlike uninhibited tendencies
- Uninhibited sexual tendencies suffer extraordinary reduction through discharge
- Sensual love becomes extinguished when satisfied unless mixed with tender components

HERD INSTINCT CRITIQUE (5 positions): HERD-001 to HERD-005
- Herd instinct postulated by Trotter as primitive and irreducible
- Herd feeling appears already in nursery as reaction-formation against jealousy
- Trotter derives social instinct from gregariousness in animals
- Man not herd animal but horde animal led by chief
- Many equals identify with one another, single person superior to all

PRIMAL HORDE THEORY (12 positions): PRIM-001 to PRIM-012
- Darwin's conjecture: primitive human society was horde ruled despotically by powerful male
- Development of totemism connected with killing of chief and transformation to brother community
- Human groups exhibit picture of superior individual among similar companions
- Psychology of group corresponds to regression to primitive mental activity of primal horde
- Group is revival of primal horde
- Primal horde may arise from any random crowd
- Group psychology is oldest human psychology
- Individual psychology emerged gradually from old group psychology
- Two kinds of psychology from first: member psychology and father/chief/leader psychology
- Members subject to ties, father of primal horde was free
- Primal father's ego had few libidinal ties
- Primal father loved no one but himself or others only as they served his needs

EGO IDEAL THEORY (14 positions): EGO-001 to EGO-014
- Ego ideal is heir to narcissism
- Ego ideal formed by influence of parents and upbringing
- Child's own self-love displaced onto ideal ego
- Ego ideal has social side as common ideal of family/class/nation
- Ego ideal binds libidinal satisfaction from parents and social demands
- Conscience is mental faculty watching over ego ideal compliance
- Self-regard depends on fulfilling ego ideal demands
- Sense of guilt arises when ego fails to satisfy ego ideal
- Ego ideal comprises sum of all limitations ego must accept
- Abrogation of ideal would be magnificent festival for ego
- Feeling of triumph when something in ego coincides with ego ideal
- Sense of guilt and inferiority express tension between ego and ego ideal
- Ego ideal can be temporarily resolved into ego
- Separation of ego ideal from ego cannot be borne for long

MELANCHOLIA AND MANIA THEORY (8 positions): MELAN-001 to MELAN-008
- Cyclical mood oscillations between depression and exalted well-being
- In mania ego and ego ideal fuse together
- Manic enjoys abolition of inhibitions, feelings of consideration, self-reproaches
- Melancholia expresses sharp conflict between ego faculties
- In melancholia ego ideal relentlessly condemns ego in delusions of inferiority
- Melancholia can occur after loss of loved object
- Lost object set up inside ego by identification and condemned by ego ideal
- Reproaches to object come to light as melancholic self-reproaches

INSTINCTS INHIBITED IN AIMS (10 positions): INHIB-001 to INHIB-010
- Directly sexual tendencies always accompanied by tender emotional ties
- Tender feeling is successor to completely sensual object tie
- Sexual ties of childhood persist though repressed and unconscious
- Instincts inhibited in aims preserve some original sexual aims
- Inhibited instincts have great functional advantage over uninhibited
- Inhibited instincts especially adapted to create permanent ties
- Inhibited instincts capable of any degree of admixture with uninhibited
- Emotional ties can easily develop into erotic wishes
- Religious ties can revert to sexual excitement
- Sexual tendencies can transform into lasting purely tender ties

SEXUAL LOVE VS GROUP TIES (6 positions): SEXG-001 to SEXG-006
- Directly sexual tendencies unfavorable to group formation
- Sexual love developed characteristics requiring limitation to two people
- Two in sexual satisfaction make demonstration against herd instinct
- More in love, more completely suffice for each other
- Violent jealousy protects sexual object-choice from group tie encroachment
- Being in love late appearance in sexual relations between men and women
